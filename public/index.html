Mapping Emergency Health Services in Senegal

Aiming to make relevant and accurate health facility information rapidly available

Read the project proposal

ABOUT THE PROJECT

In July 2020 OpenStreetmap Senegal will map all the public hospitals in Senegal and update the emergency health services available. This is part of a wider campaign of healthsites.io to define emergency health services throughout Africa.

We currently have baseline information for a limited number of Senegalese health care facilities in OpenStreetMap. We will establish building blocks for others to use and take forward. We will compare the open data from the 197 hospitals in OpenStreetMap with that of the open dataset developed by the KEMRI-Wellcome Research Programme in 2018 and currently hosted by WHO. With support from Afrimapr we will establish R building blocks to enable customised in-country analysis using open source packages. We will track progress in terms of data capturing of additional health facilities in OSM on a daily basis. For this we will use tools such as the Ohsome application developed by HeiGIT and afrihealthsites developed by afrimapr.

WHAT WAS THE STATUS AT THE BEGINNING OF THE PROJECT?

At the start of this project healthsites.io had collected information on 1,221 health facilities in Senegal with most of them based in Dakar.

Most of the health facilities available in the healthsites.io database are pharmacies (48%) with doctors (24%), clinics (14%), hospitals (10%), and dentists (3%) making up the remainder of the entries.

Only partial information is available for all facilities listed in healthsites.io. This means most entries do not list details that are critical to support humanitarian responses.

Project Plan

The project has several collaborators who will each play a role

Contact health facilities

Senegal OpenStreetMap mappers will contact hospitals via phone to confirm emergency services available

Upload data

Health facility data will be uploaded to OpenStreetMap by the mappers

Project management

healthsites.io will be responsible for project management ensuring all stakeholders are kept informed of progress and challenges

Visualise progress

The afrimapr team will be responsible for real-time visualisation of data collection progress via re-usable, opensource R code

Open data comparison

The afrimapr team will provide data analysis and comparison with other open health facility data

Open licensing

All materials, including blog posts, images, code, and data created in this project will be released under open licenses

Historical comparison

The HeiGIT team will facilitate historical comparisons with OSM data via their tool - OHSome

Share experience

The team will provide updates and lessons learned via blog posts and a project report

HOT is an international team dedicated to humanitarian action and community development through open mapping.
